About PCB

   A printed circuit board (PCB) mechanically supports and electrically connects electronic components using conductive tracks, pads and other features etched from one or more sheet layers of copper laminated onto and/or between sheet layers of a non-conductive substrate. Components are generally solder onto the PCB to both electrically connect and mechanically fasten them to it. Printed circuit boards are used in nearly all electronic products and in some electrical products, such as passive switch boxes. Alternatives to PCBs include wire wrap and point to point construction both once popular but now rarely used. PCBs require additional design effort to lay out the circuit, but manufacturing and assembly can be automated. Electronic computer aid software is available to do much of the work of layout. Mass-producing circuits with PCBs are cheaper and faster than with other wiring methods, as components are mounted and wired in one operation.
